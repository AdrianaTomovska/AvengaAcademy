Introduction to Coffee

Coffee is one of the most popular beverages in the world, enjoyed by millions of people every day for its rich flavor, energizing effects, and cultural significance. It is made from roasted seeds—commonly called coffee beans—of the Coffea plant, which grows mainly in tropical regions such as Latin America, Africa, and Asia.

From strong espresso shots to creamy lattes, coffee brings comfort, energy, and joy. Across the globe, coffee has become a symbol of community, creativity, and culture.

Espresso

A strong, concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground beans. It's the base for many other coffee drinks.

Latte

A coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk.

Cappuccino

Espresso topped with steamed milk foam, light and creamy.

Americano

Espresso diluted with hot water, creating a lighter coffee.

Coffee Fun Facts

  • Morning Booster: The world drinks over 2.25 billion cups of coffee every day!
  • Sleepy Origins: Coffee was discovered by a goat herder in Ethiopia—his goats got energetic after eating coffee cherries.
  • Espresso Math: A single shot of espresso has less caffeine than a regular cup of coffee, but packs a bigger punch!
  • Coffee Travels: Coffee beans were first roasted in Yemen before spreading to Europe.
  • Green Gold: Coffee is the second most traded commodity globally, after oil.
